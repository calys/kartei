# AGM prep – STWEG MABA, 25.03.2026

## 1. Executive summary

- **Most of the meeting is routine**: opening, quorum, vote counters, approval of last year’s minutes, annual accounts, discharge of management, committee, budget, miscellaneous.
- **The real debate is heating**. The biggest decisions are agenda items **8.1.1, 8.1.2, 8.1.3 and 8.2**.
- **8.1.1** is about replacing the **heating regulation and central distributor** because spare parts are no longer available and the current system is old.
- **8.1.2** is the strategic choice: **future gas** versus **future district heating (Fernwärme)**.
- **8.1.3** only matters **if 8.1.2 passes**. It asks whether the Fernwärme project should go ahead **without a specialist planner**.
- **8.2** is politically sensitive: it is about **disclosing the heating settings and who decides them**.
- **8.3** is minor in comparison: a **bike shelter for the commercial units**.
- **Agenda item 9** matters financially: the renovation plan shows **CHF 119’000** for the heating centre in 2026, **CHF 506’000** for windows in 2028, and **CHF 55’000** in 2029 for heat generation / connection; the renovation fund stands at **CHF 337’169** at year-end 2025 and the annual contribution shown in the planning is **CHF 110’000**.
- **Agenda item 10** matters because the 2026 budget is being aligned with these upcoming needs.
- **Important practical point for you**: if you attend before the notary signing, you are **not yet owners**. You can usually attend only as guests unless the seller gives you a **proxy (Vollmacht)**.

### Traktandum 8 – Anträge
This is the core of the meeting.

#### 8.1.1 Heizung – Erneuerung Regulierung und Verteiler
**What it is:** Vote on replacing the heating regulation and central distributor.

**What matters:**
- LFS says the current regulation is largely from the original construction period, has exceeded its life expectancy, and spare parts are no longer available.
- The proposed package is the **E2** package: regulation replacement **plus** distributor refurbishment.
- Budgeted cost: **CHF 119’000**.
- Timing in the motion: **2026** if the community stays with gas; **2027** if district heating is chosen.
- Michael Varga from LFS is expected to be present for questions.
- Voting quorum for the motions is stated as **qualified majority, value shares only, no headcount vote**.

**Key concepts:** Regulierung, Verteiler, Heizzentrale, Ausfallrisiko, Ersatzteile, Fachplaner, qualifiziertes Mehr, Wertquote.

**Most likely questions / tensions:**
- Is this work urgent or can it wait?
- Why replace the distributor as well?
- Is CHF 119’000 the real expected cost or still rough?
- If Fernwärme is chosen, why wait until 2027 for this part?

#### 8.1.2 Heizung – Ersatz durch Fernwärme
**What it is:** Strategic vote on future heat generation: district heating versus gas when the boiler reaches end of life.

**What matters:**
- LFS expects a boiler replacement around **2029**.
- District heating could be available for the **2027/28 heating season**.
- The report compares three variants and says a **gas replacement without WBG Lägern** is economically cheapest over 20 years.
- LFS’ comparison uses approx. **CHF 93’700** investment for Fernwärme and **CHF 78’000** for gas without WBG.
- Martin Meyer’s motion paper translates this into total project logic of roughly **CHF 168’000** (Fernwärme with LFS planning), **CHF 160’000** (Fernwärme via VonTobel), and **CHF 136’000** (gas via VonTobel), i.e. including the 8.1.1 baseline work.
- LFS also says district heating could receive around **CHF 44’400** in subsidies.
- LFS still concludes that, on its assumptions, gas is **materially cheaper** over 20 years, even if maintenance is slightly lower with district heating.

**Important red flag to ask about:**
- The attached Regionalwerke offers are dated **17.12.2025** and state a validity only until **17.03.2026**, which is **before the AGM**. That means the meeting may be discussing a basis that has already expired unless it has been extended.

**Key concepts:** Fernwärme, Gasersatz, Wärmeerzeugung, Förderbeiträge, Anschlussgarantie, Arbeitspreis, Grundpreis, Lebenszykluskosten, WBG Lägern.

**Most likely questions / tensions:**
- Are the Fernwärme offers still valid?
- Which total cost should owners rely on: LFS, VonTobel, or Regionalwerke?
- Should the community decide now even though the actual switch would only be in 2027?
- Is it smarter to keep gas because the law still allows it for now?
- How much weight should be given to ecological arguments versus cost arguments?

#### 8.1.3 Heizung – Installation ohne Fachplaner
**What it is:** Conditional motion that only becomes relevant if 8.1.2 passes.

**What matters:**
- Claimed saving without a specialist planner: about **CHF 8’000**.
- This is small relative to the total Fernwärme project volume.
- The obvious counter-question is risk: does saving CHF 8’000 create execution, coordination, warranty, or quality risks?

**Key concepts:** ohne Fachplaner, Koordination, Bauleitung, Kostenersparnis, Risiko.

**Most likely questions / tensions:**
- Why save on the planner on a technically sensitive project?
- Who coordinates the trades if there is no Fachplaner?
- Who is responsible if things go wrong?

#### 8.2 Offenlegung Heizungseinstellungen und wer diese bestimmt

**What matters:**

**Key concepts:** Heizungseinstellungen, Offenlegung, Betriebszeiten, Temperaturen, Entscheidkompetenz, Transparenz.

**Most likely questions / tensions:**
- Who currently decides the heating schedule and settings?
- What exactly should be disclosed?
- Is this a transparency issue, a comfort issue, or both?
- Could broader user access create operational problems?

**Key concepts:** Velounterstand, Gewerbe, Platzmangel, Nordseite, Bewohner, Mieter.
